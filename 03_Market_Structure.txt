AURIC SENTINEL
DOCUMENT 03: MARKET STRUCTURE
================================================================

1. PURPOSE

The Market Structure module is responsible for interpreting price
action through an institutional/smart-money lens: identifying structure
breaks, tracking liquidity behavior, and classifying price location
relative to premium, discount, and equilibrium zones. This module takes
the directional bias produced by the EMA Trend Engine and either
reinforces it with structural evidence or flags a conflict that the
trader should be aware of.

2. WHY THIS MODULE EXISTS

A moving-average-based trend read alone can be misleading around
turning points, since averages lag price. Market structure analysis is
intended to catch structural evidence of shifting control between
buyers and sellers earlier and more precisely than a lagging average
stack can. This module exists to give the system a structural,
price-action-based confirmation layer that complements rather than
replaces the trend engine.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

- The module must identify significant swing highs and swing lows on
  both a higher timeframe (context) and the execution timeframe, using
  a clearly defined and consistent swing detection method.
- The module must detect breaks of structure, meaning price closing
  beyond a previously established significant swing point in the
  direction of the prevailing move.
- The module must detect character changes, meaning a break of
  structure that occurs against the prevailing higher timeframe trend,
  signaling a potential shift in control.
- The module must identify and maintain zones representing areas where
  institutional order flow is presumed to have originated (commonly
  referred to as order blocks), based on the candle(s) immediately
  preceding a strong directional move that produced a structure break.
- The module must classify current price location relative to a
  defined range as being in a premium zone, discount zone, or
  equilibrium zone, using the higher timeframe range as the reference.
- The module must distinguish between higher timeframe structural
  events and execution timeframe structural events, and clearly label
  which timeframe each detected event belongs to.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

- All structural detection must be non-repainting: once a swing point,
  structure break, or order block is confirmed and displayed, it must
  never be silently removed, moved, or reinterpreted on historical
  bars.
- The module must remain visually uncluttered; not every minor swing
  should be marked. Only structurally significant points, as defined by
  the module's configurable sensitivity, should be marked.
- Zone tracking (order blocks, premium/discount) must have a defined
  invalidation rule so that outdated or violated zones are removed or
  visually marked as invalidated rather than accumulating indefinitely
  on the chart.

5. USER CONFIGURABLE SETTINGS

- Swing detection sensitivity (how many bars on each side define a
  significant swing point) for both higher timeframe and execution
  timeframe.
- Toggle to show/hide order blocks.
- Toggle to show/hide premium/discount/equilibrium zones.
- Maximum number of active order blocks to track/display at once, to
  avoid clutter.
- Option to visually distinguish higher timeframe structural elements
  from execution timeframe structural elements using distinct color
  families.
- Order block invalidation rule selection (for example, invalidated on
  a close through the zone versus a wick through the zone).

6. INPUT DESCRIPTION

Primary input is price data (high, low, close) on both the higher
timeframe used for context and the execution timeframe used for
detailed structure tracking. Secondary input is the directional bias
produced by the EMA Trend Engine, used to determine whether a given
structure break should be classified as trend-confirming or as a
character change.

7. OUTPUT DESCRIPTION

The module's output, consumed by other modules, consists of:
- The most recent structural event type (break of structure or
  character change) and its direction, per timeframe.
- The current zone classification of price (premium, discount, or
  equilibrium).
- A list or reference to currently active, unmitigated order blocks
  relevant to potential entries.
- A structural alignment flag indicating whether current structure
  agrees with, conflicts with, or is neutral relative to the EMA Trend
  Engine's bias.

8. VISUAL BEHAVIOUR

Higher timeframe structural elements should be visually distinct from
execution timeframe elements (for example, through color family,
line style, or opacity) so the trader can immediately tell which
timeframe a given structural marking belongs to. Order blocks should be
drawn as shaded zones with clear boundaries. Structure break labels
should be concise (for example, short text tags) rather than verbose,
since this module's information will also be summarized on the
Dashboard.

9. SIGNAL BEHAVIOUR

This module does not generate final entry signals. It provides
structural context and confirmation/conflict flags that the Signal
Engine (Document 04) uses as one of its qualifying conditions before
issuing an entry trigger. A character change against the prevailing
trend, for example, may be used by the Signal Engine as an early
warning to reduce confidence in trend-following signals, or as the
basis for a counter-trend signal type if the system supports one.

10. TRADING PHILOSOPHY BEHIND THIS MODULE

The underlying philosophy follows smart-money/institutional concepts:
price moves from one liquidity pool to another, structure breaks
reveal where control has shifted, and order blocks mark areas where
institutional participants are presumed to have entered positions that
may later be defended. Premium/discount zone classification is used to
avoid entries at structurally unfavorable price locations, favoring
entries from discount zones for long bias and premium zones for short
bias, with equilibrium treated as a neutral, lower-confidence zone.

11. DECISION PROCESS

On each confirmed bar, per timeframe:
1. Update the record of significant swing highs and lows.
2. Check whether the most recent confirmed close has broken beyond the
   most recent relevant swing point.
3. If broken, classify the break as trend-confirming or character
   change based on current EMA Trend Engine bias.
4. If the break qualifies under the order block detection rule, mark
   the originating candle(s) as a new order block zone.
5. Recalculate premium/discount/equilibrium boundaries based on the
   current higher timeframe range.
6. Check existing order blocks for invalidation based on subsequent
   price action.

12. EDGE CASES

- Choppy, low-volatility conditions producing frequent minor swings
  that do not represent meaningful structure: sensitivity settings must
  allow filtering these out.
- Multiple overlapping order blocks in a similar price region: the
  module should have a rule for consolidating or prioritizing the most
  relevant/recent one to avoid visual clutter.
- Gaps or missing data causing an apparent structure break that is
  actually a data artifact: the module should be robust against
  isolated single-bar anomalies where feasible.
- Very early in a symbol's history where insufficient swing data exists
  to establish a reliable structure: module must report "insufficient
  structural data" rather than fabricating a classification.

13. PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS

Structural updates should occur promptly on bar confirmation without
introducing noticeable delay, and the number of tracked zones/objects
must be bounded (via the configurable maximum) to keep the module
performant, especially when this system is used in multi-symbol
scanning mode (Document 09).

14. FUTURE EXPANSION IDEAS

- Liquidity sweep detection (marking areas where price took out prior
  highs/lows before reversing) as a distinct structural event type.
- Fair value gap / imbalance detection as a supplementary zone type.

15. DEVELOPER NOTES

- Keep higher timeframe and execution timeframe structural logic as
  parallel but separate tracking systems internally, even though they
  share the same underlying detection method, to avoid cross-
  contamination of state between timeframes.
- Ensure invalidated order blocks are clearly removed or marked, since
  stale zones lingering on the chart undermine trader trust in the
  module.

16. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

- Structure breaks and character changes are correctly classified
  relative to current trend bias.
- Order blocks are created and invalidated according to the configured
  rule, with no zones persisting indefinitely past invalidation.
- Premium/discount/equilibrium classification updates correctly as the
  higher timeframe range evolves.

17. TESTING CHECKLIST

- Verify structural markings do not repaint on historical review.
- Verify character change labeling correctly reflects conflict with
  current EMA Trend Engine bias.
- Verify order block count stays within the configured maximum.
- Verify zone classification changes appropriately as price moves
  across the range.

18. KNOWN LIMITATIONS

- Structural analysis, like all price-action interpretation, involves
  some subjectivity in swing definition; the module's output should be
  understood as one structured interpretation among several valid
  approaches, not an objective ground truth.

19. INTEGRATION REQUIREMENTS

- Requires directional bias input from the EMA Trend Engine (Document
  02).
- Output must be consumable by the Signal Engine (Document 04) and
  summarized on the Dashboard (Document 06).

20. COMMON IMPLEMENTATION MISTAKES TO AVOID

- Redrawing or moving previously confirmed structural markings, which
  breaks non-repainting requirements.
- Allowing unbounded accumulation of order block zones, degrading both
  performance and readability.
- Conflating higher timeframe and execution timeframe structural state
  into a single undifferentiated data set.

21. QUALITY ASSURANCE CHECKLIST

- All structural labels are timeframe-tagged.
- All zones have a defined and functioning invalidation rule.
- Structural alignment flag correctly reflects agreement/conflict with
  trend bias in manual spot checks across varied historical periods.

22. EXTENDED STRUCTURAL SPECIFICATIONS

This section expands Market Structure beyond basic break-of-structure
and character-change detection into a fuller structural analysis
framework. As with all sections in this documentation set, only
required behaviour, expected output, and trading relevance are
described; no detection method, formula, or code is specified.

22.1 Internal Structure

Internal Structure refers to structural behaviour occurring on a lower,
more granular timeframe within the context of a larger, established
move on a higher timeframe (for example, minor swings forming within a
single leg of a higher timeframe trend). The module must track and
report internal structure separately from External Structure (Section
22.2), since internal structural events are used primarily for
refining entry timing, while external structural events are used for
establishing overall directional context.

22.2 External Structure

External Structure refers to the larger-degree structural behaviour
that defines the overall higher timeframe trend and range context —
the swings and structural events that the EMA Trend Engine's bias and
the overall market narrative are built upon. The module must ensure
that External Structure classification remains the dominant reference
for overall bias confirmation, with Internal Structure treated as a
refinement layer subordinate to it.

22.3 Swing Classification

The module must classify every detected swing point according to its
relative significance rather than treating all swing points as equal.
At minimum, swings must be classified along two dimensions: size/
degree (Major or Minor, Section 22.4) and behavioural quality (Strong
or Weak, Section 22.5). This classification must be consistently
applied so that downstream structural events (breaks, character
changes) can be described in terms of which category of swing was
involved.

22.4 Major Swing and Minor Swing

A Major Swing is a swing point of sufficient size and significance
relative to recent price history to be considered a meaningful
reference for External Structure. A Minor Swing is a smaller, less
significant swing point primarily relevant to Internal Structure. The
module must define clear, consistent, configurable criteria
distinguishing the two categories, and must ensure that structural
events (breaks of structure, character changes) are labeled according
to which category of swing was broken, since a break of a Major Swing
carries materially different significance than a break of a Minor
Swing.

22.5 Strong Swing and Weak Swing

Independent of size classification, the module must also assess the
behavioural quality of a swing point: a Strong Swing is one formed with
clear, decisive price rejection and follow-through in the opposing
direction, while a Weak Swing is one formed with hesitant, low-
conviction price behaviour (for example, minimal follow-through or
significant overlap with surrounding price action). This quality
rating must be used to weight the reliability of any structural event
built upon that swing point.

22.6 Impulse Move

An Impulse Move is a directional price move characterized by strong,
decisive follow-through with minimal internal overlap or retracement,
typically the type of move that produces order blocks (Document 03,
Section 3) and drives structure breaks. The module must detect and
flag Impulse Moves as a distinct behavioural event, since their
presence is treated as evidence of strong directional conviction behind
a structural event.

22.7 Correction Move

A Correction Move is a retracement or consolidation move occurring
against or within the context of a larger Impulse Move or Trend Leg,
without itself breaking the prevailing structure. The module must
distinguish Correction Moves from genuine reversals, consistent with
the Pullback Detection concept defined for the EMA Trend Engine
(Document 02, Section 22.8), and must communicate this distinction
clearly to avoid the Signal Engine misinterpreting a normal correction
as a structural failure.

22.8 Trend Leg

A Trend Leg is a complete directional segment of price movement
comprised of one or more Impulse Moves and Correction Moves, bounded by
significant swing points, representing one coherent phase of the
prevailing trend. The module must track the current active Trend Leg
and report its maturity (early, mid, late) based on its duration and
extension relative to prior legs on the same instrument, since this
maturity assessment is relevant to both Trend Exhaustion (Document 02,
Section 22.13) and structural entry timing.

22.9 Market Phases

The module must classify the overall market condition into a small,
well-defined set of phases: Trending (clear directional Trend Leg
progression), Ranging (repeated structure without sustained directional
progression), Accumulation/Distribution (low-volatility consolidation
preceding a potential directional expansion, distinguished by tight,
overlapping structure near a significant prior high or low), and
Transitional (active character change without yet establishing a new
clear directional bias). This phase classification must be reported as
a distinct, prominent output, since it materially affects how
downstream modules should weight structural and signal evidence.

22.10 Structure Strength Rating

The module must synthesize swing classification, move classification,
and market phase into a single overall Structure Strength Rating,
analogous in purpose to the Trend Confidence Rating defined for the EMA
Trend Engine (Document 02, Section 22.19), expressed in plain language
for direct consumption by the Signal Engine and Dashboard.

22.11 Structure Failure

Structure Failure describes a condition where a previously established
structural bias (a sequence of higher highs/higher lows or lower highs/
lower lows) breaks down without a clean, decisive character change
occurring — that is, structure becomes disordered or choppy rather than
cleanly reversing. This must be reported as a distinct state from
Character Change (Document 03, Section 3), since it represents
structural ambiguity rather than a confirmed directional shift, and
should generally reduce confidence in both trend-following and
counter-trend signals alike until clarity returns.

22.12 Structure Confirmation

Structure Confirmation describes the condition where a detected
structural event (break of structure or character change) is validated
by sufficient follow-through price behaviour after the initial break,
as opposed to an immediate false break. The module must define a clear,
consistent confirmation requirement (for example, sustained closes
beyond the broken level, or subsequent swing formation consistent with
the new direction) before a structural event is treated as fully
confirmed and passed downstream at full confidence. Unconfirmed
structural events may still be reported, but must be clearly flagged
as provisional.

22.13 Swing Validation Rules

The module must define consistent rules for when a detected swing point
becomes "valid" and eligible to be used as a reference for structural
event detection, as opposed to a preliminary, not-yet-confirmed
candidate swing. This distinction is essential to the non-repainting
requirement established throughout this documentation set: a swing
point must not be used to justify a displayed structural event until it
meets its own validation criteria, and once validated and displayed,
must not later be invalidated retroactively.

22.14 Liquidity-Based Structure Interpretation

The module must interpret certain structural behaviours through a
liquidity lens: recognizing when price approaches or trades through a
prior significant swing point in a manner suggestive of liquidity
being targeted (for example, a brief push beyond a prior high or low
followed by swift rejection back into the prior range) as distinct from
a genuine structural break. This liquidity-oriented interpretation must
be reported as a distinct behavioural flag, since it is commonly used
to anticipate structural reversals before a formal character change is
confirmed, and should inform, but not override, the module's core
structural classification.

22.15 Interaction with Trend Engine and Signal Engine

All extended structural concepts in this section must integrate with
the rest of the system as follows:
- Market Phase (Section 22.9) and Structure Strength Rating (Section
  22.10) must be made available to the EMA Trend Engine's Multi-
  Timeframe Alignment concept (Document 02, Section 22.16) as
  supplementary context, without the Trend Engine being required to
  depend on it.
- Impulse Move, Correction Move, and Trend Leg maturity (Sections 22.6
  through 22.8) must be available to the Signal Engine (Document 04) to
  help distinguish genuine pullback-based entries from entries
  occurring during structurally ambiguous or exhausted conditions.
- Structure Failure and unconfirmed structural events (Sections 22.11
  and 22.12) must reduce, rather than block outright, the confidence
  level passed to the Signal Engine, allowing the Signal Engine's own
  decision hierarchy (Document 04) to determine whether reduced
  structural confidence is still sufficient for a lower-conviction
  signal category.
- Liquidity-Based Structure Interpretation (Section 22.14) must be
  available as an optional early-warning input to the Signal Engine for
  anticipatory, higher-risk signal categories, clearly distinguished
  from standard confirmed-structure signal categories.
